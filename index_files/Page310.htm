Introduction to Electronics

This course introduced the elementary concepts from the big electronics world. Kids were getting familiar with Electricity and Magnetism, learned how to create a simple circuit and started working with some basic electronic components such as resistors and capacitors. At a later date, more complicated devices, such as transistors and chips were introduced. Based on the learned material the Club members were able to build solar cars  and participate in a car race. Another project, which was completed during the course—members had an opportunity to build simple line-following robots utilizing a logic gate chip as a brain. These robots were introduced in the 2015 Calgary Maker Faire. The last project of this course was a Christmas Star—an ornament based on a trigger-counter chip.

Age: 10-15

This course is completed

Small Arduino bots programming

Introduction to the Arduino programming course includes a viewing of the real programming language such as C. This course presented general ideas like digital numbers, variables, conditional brunch, and loop operators. In addition, the Arduino IDE programming software was installed in students’ laptops, and they started to write first Arduino programs. In this course Club members completed the UltraSonic Ranger project and were able to build a small Arduino based exploring robot.

Age: 12-16

This course is completed.

Lego Mindstorm robots developing

Starting September 2016 our club introduces the Lego Mindstorms course. Our team will learn the concept of basic programming ideas like loops, switches (if-else operators) and variables. Students will build their own robots with motors and sensors leading to participation at the Club Lego Mindstorms competition. Near the end of the year our team will take part in the annual First Lego League championship which will start in the middle of December.

Age: 8-14

The course will start in September, 2016.
